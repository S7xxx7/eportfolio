Proof of Critical Thinking — Abnormal Psychology & Cyber Behaviour

Below I show the exact steps I used to analyze my own online presence and behaviour using concepts from Abnormal Psychology (biases, social influence) and Cyber Behaviour (digital footprints, social engineering). This is real reasoning shown as a concise audit + action plan.

Goal:

Evaluate my profiles and online behaviour, identify psychological/cyber risks, and make targeted improvements.

Process & Evidence of Thinking

  • Inventory (data collection). Made a list of all public accounts (LinkedIn, Facebook, Instagram, Twitter/X, GitHub) and identified where personal phone/email or geolocation was exposed.
  • Apply psychological concepts. Looked for signs of online disinhibition and cognitive biases: noted posts that were emotionally charged (likely to invite oversharing). Observed news sources I often repost; checked for confirmation bias.
  • Threat model for social engineering. Assessed what an attacker could learn and determined plausible social-engineering vectors.
  • Technical controls applied. Moved phone number and personal email out of public bios; put a business contact email on LinkedIn. Enabled 2FA and turned off geotagging.
  • Behavioral controls applied. Adopted pause-before-post and source-check routines.
  • Reflection & learning. Wrote a short paragraph summarizing what I found and the new habits to keep. Scheduled a quarterly audit of my digital footprint.

Conclusion (short)

Using abnormal-psychology concepts allowed me to identify why I overshare or re-share certain content, while cyber-behaviour techniques showed how that oversharing becomes an attack vector. The combined approach led to immediate, measurable safeguards (privacy removal, 2FA) and lasting behavioral habits (pause-before-post, source-check).
