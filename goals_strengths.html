Proof of Critical Thinking — Abnormal Psychology & Cyber Behaviour

Below I show the exact steps I used to analyze my own online presence and behaviour using concepts from Abnormal Psychology (biases, social influence) and Cyber Behaviour (digital footprints, social engineering). This is real reasoning shown as a concise audit + action plan.

Goal

Evaluate my profiles and online behaviour, identify psychological/cyber risks, and make targeted improvements.

Process & Evidence of Thinking

  • Inventory (data collection). I made a list of all public accounts (LinkedIn, Facebook, Instagram, Twitter/X, GitHub) and identified where personal phone/email or geolocation was exposed.
  • Psychology applied. I looked for signs of online disinhibition and cognitive bias: emotionally charged posts that invite oversharing, and confirmation bias in what I repost.
  • Threat model. I asked: “What would an attacker learn about me?” (job role, location patterns, contacts). I mapped likely attack paths like phishing or fake tech support calls.
  • Technical controls. I removed personal contact info from public bios, enabled 2FA, disabled geotagging, and deleted a few risky old posts.
  • Behavioural controls. I now use pause-before-post (wait 30 min before posting emotional content) and source-check (verify before resharing).
  • Reflection. I wrote a short summary to myself and planned a quarterly digital footprint audit.

Conclusion

Using abnormal-psychology concepts helped me understand why I overshare. Cyber-behaviour helped me control how I present myself online. The result is practical protection against social engineering and a better professional image.
